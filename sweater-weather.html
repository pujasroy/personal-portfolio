Sweater weather

| poetry

Sweater weather. Steamed baths. Mocha cravings.

Foggy breaths. Dewy leaves.

Blanket snuggles. Dry skins. Awaited sunbeams.

All of which, are delicacies

Cracking up

When winter decides to finally give a knock.

To finally let the lonesome find company

In their sweaters, in their baths, in their cups,

In their quilts, skins, etc, etc.

To let them find warmth, and love.

And peace. And solitude.

But deep down, they know,

How this comfort isn't everlasting. Nothing is perennial.

It's just a matter of days,

Until they have to let go of the warmth.

Until they have to lose it to the other hemisphere.

And struggle to fit in with the upcoming season.
