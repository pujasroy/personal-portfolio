The Duality of Existence

| poetry

Cornered from the world's perils,

To what should have been a foster,

There lied depths to 'soundness',

And grief; look, how sinister.

They preached of love,

And gathered chests of hearts,

But failed at sheltering their own;

Here— the wry misery starts.

Discussed religion, and picked up

Stories in cults, that ended in morals;

Steering clear of their cladded sins,

Still jewelling filthy webs with florals.

What kind of foster fosters pretence?

Losing brains, but calling the same sane;

Mighty dualism that makes you,

Forever indifferent to your kind's pain.

They narrow this down to normalcy,

I rebel and I rebel until I'm impelled to plead;

But who do you even find to blame,

When it's your fate that makes you bleed?
