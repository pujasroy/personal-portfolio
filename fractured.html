I see flashes of fractured ties everyday

|poetry

I see flashes of fractured ties everyday,

Bonds that once were, but now are out of existence.

Tunes which exhibited melody at a point,

Today, they make me break down at the shortest lyric.

[Occupied but] empty mirrors on the wall,

Which mourn the loss of all that once was,

But now refuses to be.

I feel helpless, hopeless,

Searching for the remnants of my past acquaintances

In every person that I meet, in every hand that I shake,

In this densely populated world,

Which does not seem to stop for anyone.

Someone said I feel things too deeply

|poetry

Someone said I feel things too deeply,

And another said I feel nothing at all.

I still perfectly carry the weight

Of two juxtaposed opinions,

Not knowing what I did to deserve either.

A person was being chased by me,

While she was busy chasing another.

It was a phase that lasted for months but

I still haven’t figured out the why

Behind the concept of chase-me-while-I-chase-her.

My secret folder

|poetry

An elderly figure taught me to chop the fruits a certain way;

To hold the fork a certain way; to lay the table a certain way.

Never did I question her manners, her way of doing things,

That she knowingly passed on to me,

That I will unknowingly pass down to another.

My secret folder still shelters all the pictures

Of me and my ex-friends, ex-lovers, ex-kins

[I always make the last term make sense].

I never had the courage to delete them,

I never wanted the courage to delete their existence.
