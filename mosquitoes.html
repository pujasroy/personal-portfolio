The mosquitoes that I swat during the day

| poetry

The mosquitoes that I swat during the day,

Get reincarnated in my nightmares.

Dragging me to the court of justice,

Then whispering their unfinished business.

I get presented before a jury of judges,

And they accuse me with certain crimes.

However when they demand a justification,

I start to freeze and make things up.

I say my blood was being sucked by them,

But the jury seems biased against me.

As if holding some age old grudge,

And wanting to charge me guilty of everything.

Without a thought, I figure out the why,

Behind their grudges and seem to try,

To creep out of the courtroom,

But get presented before them again.

Soon, I retaliate with another sharp blow,

But this time, they escape my murderous hands.

And without another unwanted justification,

They sneak back into their dugged out graves.

The menacing night passes inevitably,

With me swearing to abide by the rules.

But my intrinsic demeanor comes to play,

When in the morning, I clap to trap another again.
