In the midst of the shallowest pool

| poetry

in the midst of the shallowest pool,

i found a body drowning, screaming.

so without another thought, i plunged,

into a fate changing darkness,

to save you, a life worth nothing now.

not because i pitied a sinking soul,

but because of my underestimation

of the apparent depth of the abyss,

and my incognizance of your identity,

tempted me to stretch my hand.

the dependence of a life on myself,

lent a sudden sense of reliability.

but the very consecutive moment,

filled my conscience with regrets.

that how i should've considered myself,

not as a lifeline to be exhausted,

but as a human being, all but selfish.

never once did i think, that you'd pull me in,

that it was a part of a well planned game,

that i stepped into and lost all that i had.

if only i'd deemed my life worthy enough,

i would've been cackling and joking,

with the forsaken family that i left behind,

the one that always saw me above all.

but now that the moments are all gone,

i admit how i miss each day terribly.

i want to live again, can you hear me?

or are you still as deaf as you were that day?

shutting every door of your ears,

reluctant to hear my drowning voice.

but i often wonder things that i shouldn't,

that maybe one day you'd realize,

how important it is for a man to survive,

in order to feed the rest of his family.

or that, if you, too, are rotting in hell

not the hell that contrasts the heaven,

but the hell which forces you to see,

the family that can't see you anymore,

to hear the voice of your kids,

beyond whose reach your voice lies.

to touch the woman of your dreams,

whom you are impalpable to after death.
