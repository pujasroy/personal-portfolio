Tonight is for the fallen angels

| poetry

Tonight is for the fallen angels

Who've been left by their comrades

To starve in this cut-throat world of civic and ethics.

A place bound by scripts and manuals,

A place devoid of indulgence and compassion.

Mortals here pretend to be hospitable,

Only as long as you're found abiding

By the unsaid rules of their flawed cultures.

Families here put on a prolonged display of complaisance,

Lest you be deemed unfit for their exemplary bloodline.

Companions hold umbrellas of their companionship,

So long as they're guaranteed a share from your platter.

Kins here are duty bound to clothe their skulls with synthetic smiles,

Conditioned to conceal their authentic selves behind.

Lovers here bombard you with emotions

Till the time they're entitled to advances.

So, my dear fallen angels,

Beware of this glittery pitfall,

For all that glitters may just not be gold.
