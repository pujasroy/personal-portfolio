The GPT craze and my inexplainable aversion to it

| article

“AI will replace jobs and create jobs” — the punchline of an article dumped on earth.com, should not be evidently found appealing to the conscience of a sane audience.

As a writer with a schedule that rarely aligns with my passion for literature, I cannot get myself to fathom the tensile grip of my peers who have often found themselves romanticizing Kafka and Dostoevsky.

I hadn’t even fully recovered from the void created by accessing an E-Book when I was greeted with AI writing and refining stories on behalf of the authors.

I find my ‘Friends, Romans and countrymen’ blend into the digital spectrum of ‘GPT, Gemini and Grok’ and it breaks my heart a little each time I have to consult ChatGPT and make it decode another Gen-Z slang.

There’s a shift happening in the world right now, and I cannot get myself to like it.

Geniuses adapting to AI are being adopted by AI.

Physical labor was assumed to be at risk for a real long time but my personal take on the matter has led me to believe that AI continues to pose maximum threat to the creativity of mind.

Why should I make an effort to think if my digital friend can brainstorm and fetch ideas that no amount of personal pondering could lead me to?

This same article also quoted how researchers believe that 65% of today’s elementary school students will work in careers that don’t yet exist. Does that not invalidate the courses they’re being offered at this point of time?

Whatever ends generations of computers failed to meet are being widely realized and reimagined by the Fifth Gen. With advancements in the field of AI & ML, reasoning is being met at with a zeal that mirrors human curiosity and precision.

As open-ended as I want this piece of work to be with heavy emphasis on the rhetoric, my silly little heart will always be a hoarder of paperbacks — no swipe feels like a turned page.
